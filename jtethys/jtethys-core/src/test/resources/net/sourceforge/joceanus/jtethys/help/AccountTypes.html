AccountType

The AccountType controls the set of transactions that can occur involving an account of that type

AccountTypes are initially divided into four classes

External
These accounts do not count towards assets. Each non-external (internal) asset has a parent account external for reporting purposes. For instance, interest payments generated by a savings account are treated as being paid by the provider (parent) of the savings account.
Monetary
Current/Savings Accounts are viewed as Monetary Assets, and are included in asset reporting. A monetary account generally has an associated rate (e.g. 2.5% APR), and these rates may vary over the lifetime of the account. A set of rates can be associated with an account so that the rate applicable to the account at any particular time can be determined. The value of a Monetary asset at any time is the sum of credits to the account minus the sum of debits from the account. Interest payment debits relating to an account are viewed as debits from the parent.
Debt
CreditCards and Mortgages are viewed as Monetary Assets, albeit normally of negative value, and are included in asset reporting. The value of a Debt asset at any time is calculated the same as Monetary assets, but generally no rates are applicable to Debts.
Priced
Share Accounts and similar are viewed as Priced Assets, and are included in asset reporting. A Priced asset has the concept of units (e.g. 10 shares) and units can be credited/debited to/from the account similar to money for a monetary account. The number of units for a priced asset at any particular time is simply the sum of units credited minus the sum of units debited. A priced account has a set of prices associated with it, with each price relating to the unit price at a particular date. The value of a Priced asset at any time is the net units multiplied by the most recent price for the account.
AccountTypeClassDescription
CurrentMonetary These are the standard banking current accounts.
InstantMonetary These are standard instant access savings accounts. The distinction between these and current accounts is now strictly for reporting purposes.
RestrictedMonetary These are savings accounts that have restricted access such as notice periods for withdrawals or restricted number of withdrawals in a calendar year.
BondMonetary These are bonds that are invested over a fixed period of time and are generally not accessible during the term of the bond.
EquityBondMonetary These are bonds that deliver returns based on the performance of the stock market over a fixed period of time.
CashISAMonetary These are instant access accounts that are held in an ISA wrapper.
ISABondMonetary These are cash bonds that are held within an ISA wrapper.
TaxFreeBondMonetary These are bonds that are tax free. (e.g NS+I certificates).
CreditCardDebt These are standard credit cards.
MortgageDebt These are standard mortgage accounts.
LoanDebt These are standard loans. It covers both commercial and personal (informal) loans.
SharesPriced These are standard shares.
UnitTrustPriced These are standard UnitTrust/OEIC accounts.
UnitISAPriced These are UnitTrust accounts that are held in an ISA wrapper
LifeBondPriced These are UnitTrusts that encompass Life Insurance. They provide a lump sum upon death and have specific tax rules regarding withdrawals.
EndowmentPriced These are standard endowment policies. Since endowment policies generally come with an annual valuation, it is simpler to treat an endowment policy as a single unit with a price that is recorded each year.
AssetPriced These are assets such as a house or a car.
